Chicken Casserole

Description

Chicken casserole is a hearty and versatile dish featuring tender pieces of chicken combined with vegetables like carrots, celery, and peas, all enveloped in a creamy, savory sauce. The sauce, often made from cream of chicken soup or a homemade béchamel, is seasoned with herbs such as thyme or rosemary. The dish is typically layered with a starchy element like pasta, rice, or potatoes, making it a well-rounded meal that's baked to perfection. Topped with a crunchy layer of breadcrumbs, cheese, or crushed crackers, chicken casserole offers a delightful texture contrast. It's a warm and comforting dish, perfect for family dinners, potlucks, or meal prep. The recipe's flexibility allows for the use of various ingredients, making it easy to tailor the flavors to your taste.

Ingredients

  • A. One can of condensed cream of chicken soup
  • B. One fourth cup milk
  • C. Six skinless, boneless chicken breasts
  • D. Six slices of Swiss cheese
  • E. Two cups of herb-seasoned stuffing mix
  • F. One fourth butter, melted

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease an 8x12-inch baking dish.
  2. Whisk condensed soup and milk together in a small bowl until well combined.
  3. Cover and bake in the preheated oven until no longer pink in the center and the juices run clear, about 50 minutes. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read at least 165 degrees F (74 degrees C).
  4. Arrange chicken in the prepared baking dish. Layer each breast with a slice Swiss cheese, then spoon soup mixture over top. Sprinkle with stuffing mix and drizzle with butter.
