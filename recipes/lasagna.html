Lasagna

Description

Making Lasagna can be time-consuming but the results are well worth the effort. Lasagna is a traditional Italian dish that has become a beloved comfort food around the world. It's a type of pasta casserole made by layering sheets of pasta with a variety of fillings and sauces. The classic lasagna is known as "lasagna al forno" and is typically made with the Pasta, Sauce, Cheese and BÃ©chamel: A creamy white sauce made from butter, flour, and milk, which adds extra richness and creaminess to the dish. These layers are repeated until the dish is full, and then it's baked until the top is golden and bubbling. The result is a delicious, satisfying meal that's perfect for gatherings and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • Meat: Italian sausage and lean ground beef
  • Onion and Garlic: An onion and two cloves of garlic cooked with the meat
  • Tomato products: A can of crushed tomatoes, two cans of tomato sauce, and two cans of tomato paste.
  • Sugar: Two table spoons of white sugar
  • Spices and seasonings: Fresh parsley, dried basil leaves, salt, Italian seasoning, fennel seeds, and black pepper
  • Lasagna noodles: Store-bought or homemade lasagna noodles
  • Cheese: Parmesan, mozzarella and ricotta cheese
  • Egg: One egg to help bind the ricotta so it doesn't ooze out when cut into it

Steps

  1. Make the meat sauce.
  2. Cook the noodles
  3. Mix the ricotta cheese with an egg and some parsley
  4. Layer the lasagna accordingly
    1. Meat sauce
    2. Noodles
    3. Ricotta cheese mixture
    4. Mozzarella cheese
    5. Meat sauce
    6. Parmesan cheese
    7. Repeat the layers, then top with the remaining Parmesan.
  5. Cover with foil and bake.
  6. Let the lasagna rest before serving.
